Frustration Led to Mike D’Antoni’s Resignation

By Marisa Cannici in Featured, Knicks, Opinion, Top Story ⋅ March 15, 2012 at 10:37pm ⋅ Post a comment

Head coach of the New York Knicks Mike D’Antoni resigned Wednesday after a chronic complication with frustration.

With Linsanity in full force and All-Star Carmelo Anthony back in action, you would think it can only get better for the Knicks (although Knicks fans knew that wasn’t the case).

For D’Antoni it came down to one thing: his All-Star players weren’t playing by his style of play.

The now-former Knicks coach D’Antoni played by a style of plays that included a fast-paced system that relies on the point-guard to be the main handler of the ball and most importantly whoever is open gets to take the shots. Playing with the same system in Phoenix, D’Antoni believed this system really worked and didn’t budge to acquire his All-Stars.

His All-Stars, Anthony and Amare Stoudemire, failed to implement the system D’Antoni was pushing. The fact that Anthony has been a scorer his entire life conflicted with that of coach’s system, which calls for a fast-paced offense with A LOT of ball movement.

The Knicks weren’t about to give up on the star they’d traded so much to get just last year, and that meant D’Antoni would be the one who needed to go.

D’Antoni’s History In NY

In May of 2008, D’Antonio left the Phoenix Suns, a team he led to four straight postseason appearances before signing a 4-year, $24 million contract with the Knicks. In his first year in New York, he achieved a 29-53 record, ending the season without a shot at playoffs. After another tough season, he managed to pick up Suns All-Star forward Amar’e Stoudemire before last year.

In 2011, fans were both stunned and excited when the Knicks traded several players to acquire Anthony and Chauncey Billups from the Nuggets. That year, the Knicks made it to their first playoffs with D’Antoni, but were swept in the first round by the Celtics.

Then on February 4 of this year, D’Antoni inserted Jeremy Lin into the starting lineup while Anthony was out with an injury. Lin opened his career with 25 points and helped spark a 7-game winning streak.

The End

That takes us to Wednesday, when in the midst of a 6-game losing streak and in third place in the Atlantic Division at 18-24, D’Antoni stepped down.

In other words, the Knicks began falling apart and their head coach resigned.

The coach told the Knicks interim general manager Glen Grunwald that he didn’t feel he could “positively affect the team” anymore, specifically Anthony.

The position will be succeeded by Mike Woodson who will serve as the interim coach for the New York Knicks. Does he have what it takes to save the Knick’s season?
