C’s Fantasy Hoops Assist: Week of (11/4-11/10)

November 11, 2007 at 1:43am in Celtics, Fantasy
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

The C’s Fantasy Hoops Assist is a weekly column devoted to the fantasy aspect of the game of basketball, specifically relating to the Boston Celtics.

This week, the Celtics went 4-0 to improve to 5-0 overall, so you can imagine that the C’s are getting good statistical contributions across the board. Believe it or not, the C’s are the only undefeated team remaining in the NBA. The team’s overall play, however, means nothing for Fantasy Basketball. Here are the top five Celtics doing the most for your fantasy team.

(Rankings based on Yahoo! default scoring system – FG%, FT%, 3PTM, PTS, REB, AST, STL, BLK, TO)
1. Kevin Garnett

  • KG loves to fill the stat sheet every night. This season, Garnett has provided positive numbers in 7 of the 9 categories. He hurts you in 3PTM (he doesn’t get very many of those) and he’s been a turnover MACHINE this season, averaging 4.2 per game. He could get more 3s as the season goes on, and his turnovers should go down, so he could get even better. As far as his positive categories, I’d say his steals numbers (2.8) probably will go down, but his overall numbers will remain consistent as he will be a fantasy force all season. There’s a reason he’s ranked as the No. 1 overall player in Yahoo!.

Celtics Fantasy Preview

October 5, 2007 at 3:32am in Celtics, Fantasy
KC Downey writes
7 comments

This season, the Celtics will be aiming for their first NBA Championship since 1986. Players will be playing for that one goal of owning a ring. What they probably don’t care much about is their individual statistics. Generally, unselfish teams make it all the way. In fantasy basketball, you need to find the most selfish players with the best statistics. That’s because you want to win the fantasy championship. It may not be as glorious as the NBA Title, but it still gives you bragging rights for a year.

The Celtics have a few players worthy of fantasy consideration in drafts, and I will profile the top five fantasy players on the Celtics, and measure them upon the nine default categories in Yahoo! fantasy leagues (FG%, FT%, 3PTM, PTS, REB, AST, ST, BLK, TO).

1. Kevin Garnett – SF, PF (possibly C)
Garnett is ranked as the No. 1 overall player in Yahoo! fantasy leagues, and why not. His play will probably elevate playing alongside two superstars. I envision his points will be a little down (still over 20 ppg), but his shooting percentages, assists, steals, and rebounds could improve simply based on the players around him. The C’s are short of talented big men, so his work on the glass will be utterly important to the C’s. One plus with KG in fantasy is that he could see enough time at center this season to be eligible for the position.
Draft Position: KG will be a top 3 pick this season. Also in the mix are LeBron James, Shawn Marion, and Kobe Bryant.
Strong Categories: FG%, FT%, PTS, REB, AST (for his position), BLK
Weak Categories: 3PTM, TO

August 28, 2007 at 12:57am in Fantasy
Pete Rampino writes
4 comments

It was that time of year again. Every year the same group (roughly the same, at least one is always coming and/or going) of fantasy football experts gather and compete in the same league. The league is run by SportsofBoston’s own KC Downey. This league started in 2004 and had been something I look forward to every year.

This is a 12 team league and the set up is different from a usual Yahoo default league. Most notable is the roster sizes. In our league we have positions of QB,QB,RB,RB,WR,WR,WR,TE along with DEF and K. We also have six bench spots.

This was the first time I did any sort of fantasy draft in person. True, only three of us could actually get together in the flesh, I was still excited. It was myself, and fellow SportsofBoston writers Dev Lutchman and KC Downey. Other writers from SportsofBoston that are in this league are Jeff Christenbury, Dan Pitrowiski, and the man behind the scenes, Chris Brickley.
