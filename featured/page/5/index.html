Add a comment

Well, I promised this Knicks season would get weird. It did, and now it’s getting even weirder. Coach Mike D’Antoni resigned Wednesday, finally fed up with the lack of control he had over the team, his players, his roster – pretty much everything. It is unclear who exactly is “running” the Knicks right now, since they have a temporary GM and an absentee owner and a Frankenstein collection of superstars and role players.

D’Antoni was smart to get out while he could. Rumors swirled that he tried to force owner James Dolan to trade Carmelo Anthony to the Nets for Deron Williams, which would have been a bizarre move for sure. Carmelo has gotten a LOT of negative press recently, mostly because he seemingly screwed up the beautiful Linsanity that swept the world just two weeks ago. Of course, Linsanity happened to coincide with the cupcake portion of the Knicks schedule (as I noted) and ended just about the same time the Knicks had to play the Bulls, Celtics, Spurs, Mavericks – it was bound to end sometime. The season is coming to an end, and so are the hopes of the Islanders making the playoffs. Once a .500 team, the Isles are now stuck at the bottom of the conference, trying to get to that last eighth spot. The Islanders are competing against talent such as Winnipeg, Tampa Bay, Buffalo, Toronto and Carolina. The only team under the Islanders are the Montreal Canadiens.

March 9, 2012 at 12:39am in Featured, Mets, Top Story
Evan S. Schwartz writes
Add a comment

The Mets have a chance to accomplish something special this year: They may become the worst baseball team from a major city in the last 20 years.

Now, there have been bad teams from Chicago and Los Angeles, there have been disappointing squads out of Boston and the Bay Area, and even those cross town Yankees have faltered here and there. But the Mets are about to complete one of the most stunning falls-from-grace in recent baseball memory, a fall that is baffling by modern MLB standards. A team that, just a few years ago, had a top-five payroll, brand new stadium, owns its own network and is from the biggest metropolitan area in the country may be the least talented, least promising franchise in all the land.
