NBA Players And Owners Meet At Negotiating Table

September 1, 2011 at 3:20pm in Celtics, NBA, News, Now!
Brian Moller writes
Add a comment

NBA owners and players met Wednesday for about six hours in an attempt to get the proverbial ball rolling at the negotiating table and hopefully lay the ground work for a new CBA. This was the first bargaining session since August 1 and only the second since the lockout was imposed.

Union President and Lakers guard Derek Fisher said, “Everyone loses if we don’t reach an agreement, that’s something that I think has always been understood.”

Knicks Hire Mike Woodson As Assistant Coach; D’Antoni Hot Seat Watch In Full Effect

August 30, 2011 at 7:01pm in Featured, Knicks
Argun M. Ulgen writes
3 comments

The 443 members of the “Fire Mike D’Antoni” Facebook Community will be pleased to learn that the New York Knicks hired former head coach of the Atlanta Hawks Mike Woodson as an assistant coach Monday. The hiring of an assistant coach in the NBA does not usually promise major reprucussions. However, the Knicks’ addition of Woodson could greatly improve the Knicks’ woeful defense (the Knicks were 22nd in defensive efficiency and 28th in points allowed in the 2010-’11 season). Moreover, if the Knicks don’t show market improvements in the upcoming season, current Knicks head coach Mike D’Antoni – who is currently under a one-year contract with the Knicks – may find himself out of a job before season’s end, with Woodson likely to fill in at least for the interim. It was a decent start to the Carmelo Anthony era in New York, but the future is much brighter.
