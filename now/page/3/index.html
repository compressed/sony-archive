“It’s Impossible Not to Believe in Tebow”

By Mike Lupica of the Daily News [Monday, 12/13/11]

Sports Reporter Mike Lupica resides in the pantheon of American sports journalism because he usually separates himself from sensationalist press while providing deftly written, well-reasoned, and cogent perspectives on myriad controversial topics.  However, even a savvy media vet like Mike Lupica decided to chug, and then spray out the Tim Tebow Kool-Aid across New York City in his Daily News column “It’s Impossible Not to Believe in Tebow.”

Lupica’s piece is nothing short of class A sensationalist journalism – a gaudy sermon that equates Denver Broncos quarterback’s faith to pre-destined victory in a football contest and to purity.  Meanwhile, the piece ignores various key facts and insights making no mention of Tebow’s terrible performance during the first three quarters of every NFL football game he has started, or of what may happen once opposing teams figure out how to properly adjust for Tebow’s play at the end of games. Derek Jeter is One of the 10 Most Fascinating People of 2011?

December 2, 2011 at 12:42pm in Featured, Now!, Top Story, Yankees
timl writes
Add a comment

Derek Jeter is one of the 10 Most Fascinating People of 2011, at least according to journalist and gargoyle, Barbara Walters. While I am a Yankee fan and a Derek Jeter fan, this might be the biggest outrage since his Gold Glove in 2010.

Jeter is a Hall-Of-Famer, one of the best Yankee players ever, and possibly the best shortstop ever. He couldn’t be less fascinating. He isn’t the most fascinating person on his team. He isn’t even the most fascinating on the left side of the infield. He is notorious for being one of the worst interviews in sports since he never gets personal. Sure he’s dated hot women, but he’s private. He’s not going to divulge intimate secrets about Minka Kelly, joke about how crazy Mariah Carey was, or tell us the difference between Jessica Alba and Jessica Biel.
