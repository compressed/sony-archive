An ‘Entourage’ of Loyalty

September 6, 2011 at 8:52pm in Featured, Now!
Demetri Raftopoulos writes
2 comments

The Beginning of an Era

Entourage has not only become a long running, popular television show. It has become a cultural phenomenon.

The show revolves around four New Yorkers who have moved to California to realize talent and a dream. This sounds like something of an upcoming pitcher moving through the minors or a college basketball star entering the draft early. But…no, one of the four main characters is not getting called up to play for the Angels or getting drafted by the Lakers.

Vincent Chase, movie star, brother, and best friend, is the main focus of Entourage and moves to Hollywood with his two best friends, Eric and Turtle, and brother, Johnny Drama, to pursue an acting career. Entourage has remained successful with the help of Vincent’s agent, Ari Gold, who provides the show with a loud and obnoxious but hysterical character that has left viewers waiting to see what he will do next.  Through ups and downs,  success and failure, despite all the glamour, cars, and girls, Entourage has truly been a story of people just trying to make it in this world. It has been about waiting to hear your name called and seeing if you made it. It has simply been about whether you are good enough to take it to the next level. Yankee Stadium Gets a “Lord of the Rings” Makeover

September 3, 2011 at 1:40am in Now!, Yankees
Abigail Miskowiec writes
One comment

If I were to design a mock Yankee Stadium based on a sci-fi trilogy, I would think the Death Star would best fit the “Evil Empire” but on Friday, Baseball Prospectus published a new look for Yankee Stadium inspired by Middle Earth of JRR Tolkien’s “Lord of the Rings” series. It’s a creative rendering that both taps into the Yankees’ rich history and highlights what makes the 2011 squad special.

I personally love the addition of “The Narcissist’s Corner” in the realm of A-Rod. Also, the outrageously spacious left field is touted as “where the gardner roams.” Ironically, my trips to the “Haunted Forest” of Yankee Stadium have turned up some of the most interesting and personable Yankees fans I’ve encountered. There are no ghosts up there, from what I can tell.

What do you think of this take on the stadium? Can you think of a better sci-fi realm for Yankee Stadium? How about Fenway?
