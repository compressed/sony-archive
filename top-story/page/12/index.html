Giants Face Elimination Early in 2011

December 11, 2011 at 9:36am in Featured, Giants, Top Story
Abigail Miskowiec writes
Add a comment

Last week the Giants proved they were capable of hanging with the best team in football. This week they’ll have to prove they can win when it counts. A loss this Sunday will squash any momentum from the Week 13 thriller against the Packers. A win will earn them a share of the division lead going into the final quarter of the season.

Player to Watch For: Mario Manningham

The Giants haven’t had much trouble in the passing game this season. Victor Cruz, the breakout star, is fourth in receiving yards, and the team has the fourth-best passing offense. Things should get even better when Mario Manningham returns from a knee injury. Manningham has three touchdowns in his four full games this season.

Success on the Ground Leads to Success in the Standings

With Ahmad Bradshaw back to at least limited action, the Giants are at close to full strength offensively. Here is where we see how good they can be. Bradshaw has only rushed for over 100 yards once this year, and that was against the less-than-stellar Buffalo Bills. The Cowboys feature an above-average rush defense so success on the ground will be key for the Giants.

Giants Stand in the Way of Undefeated Packers

December 4, 2011 at 1:28pm in Featured, Giants, Top Story
Abigail Miskowiec writes
Add a comment

The Giants may be the last team standing between Aaron Rodgers and a perfect season. If the Pack slip by Big Blue, they will face Oakland, Kansas City, Chicago and Detroit in the last four games of the season. Three of those will be at Lambeau Field. Manning and Co. have a tall task in stopping the Packers today, but it just might be that history and fate are on New York’s side.

Is Bradshaw Back?

In Ahmad Bradshaw’s absence, the Giants have tumbled from the division lead and are riding a three-game losing streak. Their rushing attack is the worst in the league, and Brandon Jacobs has become prone to running east-west with no gain. Eli Manning is back to throwing picks.

There is good news! Bradshaw says he will play today, and the mere threat of his presence may disrupt the already shaky Packers defense. The options that Bradshaw presents out of the backfield could be enough to turn the tide of the game and settle Manning down in the pocket.
